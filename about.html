About Me

I started college as a Psychology student but only because I never really knew what I wanted to do and I just thought the field of Psychology was cool. I continued to study Psychology for the next 3 years. After entering my senior year I realized that continuing on to get my masters in counseling/therapy was not something I wanted to do. Fortunately, I had a variety of relationships and connections that put me towards the path of getting a Computer Science degree.

After my first couple CS courses I started to really love it. Even though I knew I wasn't very good at it (yet) I could see a future in the field. One of my most influential moments was taking my homework to a professor knowing I was close to solving it but not seeing the issue I was having. He looked at it for a total of 5 seconds and changed a < to > and I said "You're smart" to which he replied "No, I've just made more mistakes then you". After that, I was far less afraid of making mistakes as it's just part of growing in your field.
